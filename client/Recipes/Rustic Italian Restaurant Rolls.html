Rustic Italian Restaurant Rolls

Breads (Yeast)

Cook Time: 12 minutes Servings: Yield: 16 Rolls Source: fifteenspatulas.com

Ingredients

2 1/4 tsp Yeast (1 packet)

1 tsp Sugar

1 1/2 cup Water (110˚)

————————

1 1/2 cup Bread Flour

180 g


1 cup Tipo 00 Flour

120 g


1/2 cup Semolina Flour

84 g


1/2 tsp Kosher Salt

————————

1 TBL Olive Oil

2 TBL Brown Butter (optional, melted)

1/4 cup Bread Flour (optional)

30g


Directions

1 In a prep cup or bowl, combine the water, yeast, and sugar. Give it a stir and let it sit for 10 minutes, until the liquid gets bubbly.

2 Whisk flours in standing mixer bowl and make uniform

3 Add yeast mixture and olive oil to dry ingredients. Optionally, add 2 TBL of Brown Butter for a hint of nutty flavor. Add an extra 1/4 cup (30g) of Bread Flour to balance the hydration if using Butter.

4 Fit the mixer with the dough hook and knead on medium low speed for 8-12 minutes. The dough should be a little sticky. The consistency you are looking for is that you can touch it, it will try to stick to you, but it will want to stick to itself more and leave a minimal amount of dough on your finger

5 For the first rise, cover the bowl with a lid or plastic wrap and let it sit for 45-60 minutes or until the dough doubles in size.

6 For the second rise, lightly grease silicon muffin trays and sprinkle bottom with corn meal to prevent sticking. Knock the air out of the risen dough and split into 45g balls (about 1/4 cup size). Place dough balls into silicon muffin tray. You should get about 16 from the recipe. Cover and let rise for 45-60 minutes or until the dough doubles in size.

7 Preheat oven to 425˚. Cook on top rack for 7-8 minutes. Take out of the oven and carefully flip rolls upside down and put back in the oven to toast the bottoms for a remaining 3 minutes.
